Sara Lieber

Research Scientist

Psychology Department at the University of Oregon

I’m a PhD student in the Social Psychology Program at the University of Oregon. My research focuses on using psychological interventions to promote environmentally-responsible consumer behaviors. I have been teaching undergraduate- and graduate-level statistics courses for nine years. I enjoy giving presentations on data visualization, network analysis, and machine learning.

I’m very interested in contributing to the betterment of science by participating in Open Science practices. This means pre-registering study methodologies and hypotheses (here and here) and making analyses available.
